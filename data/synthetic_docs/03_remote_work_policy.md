# Remote Work Policy

**Policy Number:** HR-2024-08
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2024
**Last Revised:** October 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Sarah Johnson, COO
**Approval:** Executive Committee

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy outlines Unify Consulting's approach to remote work arrangements, ensuring flexibility for employees while maintaining operational excellence and client service quality.

**Scope:** This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of Unify Consulting.

---

## 2. Remote Work Philosophy

Unify Consulting embraces a hybrid work model that balances:
- **Flexibility:** Empowering employees to work where they're most productive
- **Collaboration:** Maintaining team cohesion and company culture
- **Client Focus:** Ensuring availability and responsiveness to client needs

We believe that trust, accountability, and clear communication enable successful remote work.

---

## 3. Eligibility

### 3.1 General Eligibility
All employees are eligible for remote work arrangements after completing 90 days of employment and demonstrating:
- Consistent performance meeting or exceeding expectations
- Effective communication and self-management skills
- Reliable home internet connection and appropriate workspace

### 3.2 Role-Specific Considerations
- **Consultants:** Must be available for client meetings (virtual or in-person) as required
- **Engineering:** May work fully remote with bi-annual on-site requirements
- **Client Success:** Must be available during client business hours (typically 9 AM - 6 PM in client timezone)
- **Business Development:** Expected to travel for client meetings as needed

---

## 4. Remote Work Arrangements

### 4.1 Hybrid Model (Default)
- Employees may work remotely up to 4 days per week
- Required in-office days: Varies by team (typically Tuesdays and Thursdays)
- In-office requirements may be adjusted based on project needs

### 4.2 Fully Remote (With Conditions)
Employees may request fully remote arrangements if:
- Living >50 miles from nearest Unify office
- Role does not require regular in-person client interactions
- Manager approval and demonstrated remote work success

**Requirements for fully remote employees:**
- Attend bi-annual company offsites (2-3 days each)
- Travel to office for quarterly team meetings
- Be available during core collaboration hours (10 AM - 3 PM ET)

### 4.3 Temporary Remote Arrangements
Short-term remote work (1-4 weeks) may be approved for:
- Personal circumstances (e.g., caring for family member)
- Travel while working
- Home relocation

---

## 5. Work Environment Requirements

### 5.1 Technology Requirements
Employees working remotely must have:
- High-speed internet (minimum 25 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- Dedicated, quiet workspace
- Company-provided laptop and security software
- Access to collaboration tools (Slack, Zoom, Google Workspace)

### 5.2 Equipment and Expenses
**Company-Provided:**
- Laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse
- Collaboration software licenses
- VPN access for secure connections

**Employee-Provided:**
- Home internet service
- Office furniture (desk, chair)

**Reimbursable Expenses (with receipt, max $50/month):**
- Upgraded internet service (if needed for work)
- Office supplies (pens, notebooks, printer ink)

### 5.3 Security Requirements
All remote employees must:
- Use VPN when accessing company systems
- Secure physical workspace when handling confidential information
- Never use public Wi-Fi for accessing client data
- Report any security incidents immediately
- Complete annual security training

---

## 6. Communication and Availability

### 6.1 Core Hours
All employees must be available during core collaboration hours:
- **Eastern Time:** 10 AM - 3 PM ET
- **Other Timezones:** Equivalent to 10 AM - 3 PM ET

### 6.2 Communication Expectations
- **Response Time:**
  - Slack messages: Within 2 hours during business hours
  - Email: Within 4 hours during business hours
  - Client messages: Within 1 hour during business hours

- **Status Updates:**
  - Update Slack status when stepping away (lunch, meetings, etc.)
  - Set calendar availability accurately
  - Notify team of planned PTO at least 2 weeks in advance

### 6.3 Video Requirements
- Camera on for all client meetings
- Camera on for internal team meetings (unless bandwidth issues)
- Camera optional for 1:1 meetings (at participants' discretion)

---

## 7. Performance and Accountability

### 7.1 Performance Metrics
Remote work performance is evaluated based on:
- Quality and timeliness of work deliverables
- Client satisfaction and feedback
- Team collaboration and communication
- Availability during core hours

### 7.2 Check-ins and Feedback
- Weekly 1:1s with direct manager
- Monthly team meetings (virtual or in-person)
- Quarterly performance reviews

### 7.3 Performance Issues
If remote work performance concerns arise:
1. Manager discusses concerns with employee
2. Performance improvement plan established (if needed)
3. Remote work privileges may be modified or suspended
4. Return to full-time in-office may be required

---

## 8. Client Considerations

### 8.1 Client Preferences
- Some clients may require on-site presence for projects
- Employees must be flexible to accommodate client needs
- Remote work arrangements may be temporarily suspended for client engagements

### 8.2 Client Communication
- Remote work arrangements do not affect client availability
- Always respond to clients within agreed SLAs
- Never use "working from home" as an excuse for delays

---

## 9. Time Tracking and Overtime

### 9.1 Time Tracking
- All employees must track time accurately (consultant timesheet system)
- Record client-billable vs. internal time
- Submit timesheets weekly by Friday 5 PM ET

### 9.2 Overtime
- Non-exempt employees must obtain approval before working overtime
- Overtime is paid at 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40 per week
- Remote work does not change overtime policies

---

## 10. Travel and Expenses

### 10.1 Required Travel
Fully remote employees must cover their own costs for:
- Bi-annual company offsites (travel to be booked 30+ days in advance)
- Quarterly team meetings

**Company covers:**
- All client-related travel
- Hotel and meals during required company events

### 10.2 Travel Booking
- Use company travel portal for all work-related bookings
- Book economy class for flights under 4 hours
- Stay within $200/night hotel budget (varies by city)

---

## 11. International Remote Work

### 11.1 U.S.-Based Employees Traveling Internationally
- Temporary international remote work (up to 30 days/year) allowed with approval
- Must maintain availability during U.S. business hours
- Company laptop and VPN required
- Compliance with local laws is employee's responsibility

### 11.2 Permanent International Remote Work
- Not currently supported due to tax and legal complexities
- Special exceptions require CEO and legal approval

---

## 12. Policy Review and Modifications

- Annually by HR and Executive Committee

Modifications require Executive Committee approval.

## 13. Requesting Remote Work Arrangements

### 13.1 Process
1. Employee discusses remote work preferences with direct manager
2. Employee submits formal request via HR portal
3. Manager reviews and approves/denies within 5 business days
4. HR documents arrangement and adds to employee file

### 13.2 Trial Periods
- First-time fully remote arrangements start with 90-day trial
- Trial extended or made permanent based on performance

---

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I work from a coffee shop?**
A: Yes, for general work. No, for client calls or confidential data work. Always use VPN.

**Q: What if my internet goes down while working remotely?**
A: Notify your manager immediately. Go to nearest Unify office or co-working space. Use mobile hotspot if needed (expense reimbursable).

**Q: Can I work remotely while traveling for personal reasons?**
A: Yes, with manager approval and as long as work quality and availability are maintained.

**Q: What if I move to a different state?**
A: Notify HR immediately. Some states have different tax and legal requirements. We'll work with you to make it happen, but may require processing time.

**Q: Do remote workers get the same benefits?**
A: Yes, all benefits are the same regardless of work location.
